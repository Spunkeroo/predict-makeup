Makeup for Beginners 2026: Complete Starter Guide

Learning makeup doesn't require a cosmetology degree or a $500 Sephora haul. This guide walks you through the essentials — what to buy, how to apply it, and where to learn in Chicago. Start with 5 products and master the basics before expanding your collection.

The 5-Product Starter Kit ($50 Total)

1. Tinted Moisturizer with SPF ($14): e.l.f. Camo CC Cream SPF 30. Lightweight coverage that evens skin tone without looking cakey. 20 shades. The SPF protects from UV even on cloudy Chicago days (UV penetrates clouds). Apply with fingers, blend outward from center of face.

2. Concealer ($7): Maybelline Instant Age Rewind. Dab under eyes in an inverted triangle shape, blend with ring finger. Covers dark circles from late Chicago nights. The sponge applicator makes it foolproof for beginners.

3. Mascara ($11): Maybelline Lash Sensational Sky High. One coat for natural, two coats for drama. Wiggle the wand at the base of lashes and pull through to tips. Look down into a mirror for the bottom lashes.

4. Lip Product ($8): Clinique Almost Lipstick in Black Honey ($21) or NYX Butter Gloss in Tiramisu ($5). Sheer color that's impossible to mess up. Apply from center of lips outward. Black Honey looks good on literally every skin tone.

5. Blush ($5): e.l.f. Putty Blush in Bali. Cream formula blends with fingers — no brush needed. Smile and dab onto the apples of your cheeks, blend upward toward temples. Cream blush looks more natural than powder for beginners.

Application Order

  1. Skincare (moisturizer, SPF) — let absorb 2 minutes
  2. Tinted moisturizer — fingers or sponge, blend outward
  3. Concealer — under eyes, any blemishes
  4. Blush — apples of cheeks
  5. Mascara — top lashes first, then bottom
  6. Lip color — last step

Learn in Chicago

  • Sephora Free Classes — Multiple Chicago locations offer free 15-minute mini makeovers and group classes. Book online, no purchase required
  • MAC Cosmetics — Michigan Avenue and Oak Brook. Free makeup consultations with professional artists
  • Chicago School of Makeup — Professional courses but they offer beginner workshops for $75-$150

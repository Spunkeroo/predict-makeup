Best Setting Sprays

How to Apply

Primer Application

  1. Start with moisturized, sunscreened skin (wait 2 minutes for sunscreen to set)
  2. Apply a pea-sized amount of primer to fingers
  3. Press and pat into skin — don't rub. Focus on large pore areas (nose, cheeks, forehead)
  4. Wait 60 seconds for primer to set before applying foundation

Setting Spray Application

  1. Complete your entire makeup look including powder
  2. Hold spray 8-10 inches from face
  3. Spray in an X pattern, then a T pattern — this ensures full coverage
  4. Do NOT rub or touch your face. Let it air dry for 30-60 seconds
  5. For maximum hold: spray, let dry, then spray a second coat

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need both primer and setting spray?

For maximum wear, yes. Primer creates the base grip and setting spray locks everything in place. Together they can extend makeup wear from 4 hours to 12+ hours. If you can only buy one, a setting spray has a bigger impact.

Can I use setting spray without foundation?

Yes. Setting spray locks in any makeup including concealer, powder, blush, and eyeshadow. It also helps blend powder products for a more natural, less cakey appearance.

What is the difference between setting spray and fixing spray?

Setting spray extends the wear time of makeup. Fixing spray (like MAC Fix+) melts powders together for a natural finish but does not significantly extend wear time. They serve different purposes.

Is drugstore primer as good as high-end?

The e.l.f. Poreless Putty Primer at $10 genuinely performs comparably to the Smashbox Photo Finish at $39 in blind comparison tests. Drugstore primers have improved dramatically.

How do I choose between matte and dewy setting spray?

Oily skin: always matte. Dry skin: dewy or natural. Combination skin: matte in summer, natural in winter. For photos and events, matte photographs better under flash.
